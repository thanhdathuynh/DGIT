User Instructions

1. Performing a Search

Navigate to the Search page.
Enter a gene name, drug name, or protein in the search box.
Select the type (Gene, Drug, or Protein) from the dropdown menu.
Click Search to view interactions and related information.

2. Using the AI Assistant

Click the chat icon in the bottom right corner to open the AI Assistant.
Type your question about genes, drugs, or proteins.
The AI will provide detailed information and context.

3. Viewing Interaction Details

Click on any drug or protein name in the results table.
The AI Assistant will automatically open with detailed information.
Review interaction scores in the visualization chart.

Key Terms

1. Gene

A segment of DNA that carries instructions for making proteins or functional RNA, influencing traits and how the body responds to drugs.

2. Protein

A molecule made of amino acids that performs essential functions in the body, such as building structures, enabling chemical reactions, and regulating processes.

3. Drug-Gene Interaction

The relationship between a pharmaceutical compound and a specific gene, which can affect drug efficacy, metabolism, and potential side effects.

4. Interaction Score

A numerical value (0-10) representing the strength of evidence for a drug-gene interaction, based on published research and clinical data.

5. MDD (Major Depressive Disorder)

A mental health condition characterized by persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest. DGIT focuses on genes, proteins, and drugs associated with MDD research.

6. Interaction Types

Inhibitor: A drug that decreases or blocks the activity of a gene or protein, preventing it from performing its normal function. This can reduce unwanted biological processes or symptoms.
Activator: A drug that increases or enhances the activity of a gene or protein, promoting its normal function and amplifying beneficial biological responses.
Drug Target: A gene or protein that a drug is designed to interact with to produce a therapeutic effect. The drug may inhibit, activate, or otherwise modulate the target's activity.
